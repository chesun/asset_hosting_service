Part 2 Instructions

Part 2 Overview

In Part 2, you will be answering the same kind of questions you did in Part 1 - you will answer 3 questions regarding each Worker Group. However, this time you can learn about the workers in the groups by sampling workers from them.

 

 

Part 2 Details

  1. In order to help you make more accurate guesses in this part, you can "sample" from each of the worker groups. For each worker group, we will show you a button like the one below. Each time you click the button, it will randomly draw one worker from the corresponding Worker Group and show you which interval (1 to 3, 4 to 6, 7 to 9, and 10 to 12) the worker's score is in. This won't affect your earnings -- it's just a chance to learn about workers in each group.
    Example: 

    In the example above, you have clicked to draw a worker from Group W and the computer randomly drew a worker who scored between 4 and 6 points (shown in red).
  2. You can sample from each Worker Group as many times as you like. However, there is a 2 second "cool down" period each time after you sample before you can click the button again. This is to make sure you take the time to understand the information.
  3. Each time you sample, the computer will put the sampled worker back into the Worker Group before you sample again.
  4. The information you learn in Part 2 will be helpful for completing your tasks better in Part 3.
  5. At the end of the experiment, we will randomly select 20% of participants to be paid a bonus based on their choices.
  6. If you are selected to be paid a bonus, we will randomly select one question from the survey and use your answer to determine your bouns. If you are selected to be paid a bonus, and the selected question is in Part 2:
    1. If the question is to guess the number of workers with each possible score:
      One of your answers in this question is randomly selected, and you are paid a $10 bonus if your answer is within ±2 of the correct answer. Otherwise you are paid $0.
    2. If the question is to guess the average score/age:
      You are paid a $10 bonus if your answer is within ±2 of the correct answer. Otherwise you are paid $0.
