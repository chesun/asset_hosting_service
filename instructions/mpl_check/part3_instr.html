Instructions

Overview: In this part, you’ll consider hiring decisions for a set of workers. After reading these instructions, you’ll answer a short comprehension quiz (2 attempts per question). Then, you’ll make your hiring choices that can affect your bonus.

  1. The Workers:
    1. We recruited 100 Prolific workers from the U.S. who completed a timed math test: 12questions in 6 minutes. To see an example of the types of math questions they answered, click here. Each worker has a nickname (anonymized to protect privacy). You will be making choices about these workers throughout this survey.
    2. Each worker's score is the number of questions they answered correctly (out of 12). For example, a worker who answered 5 questions correctly would have a score of 5. No one had 0 points.
  2. Random Worker Profiles: You will see 8 workers, each randomly chosen from our group (each worker has an equal chance of being selected).

    Each worker’s profile includes:
    • A nickname
    • A profile avatar
    • A short description
    • Some profiles include the worker's score range, others do not

    Example Profile:

  3. Your Task: For each worker, you will make 12 independent choices, each shown in a separate row. For each row, you will see two options:
    • Hire this worker (option 1, on the left), and earn $1 for every point the worker scored (e.g. if the worker scored 6, you earn $6; if the worker scored 8, you earn $8, etc.).
    • Don't hire this worker (option 2, on the right), and earn a fixed amount of money, which starts at $1 in the first row and increases by $1 in each subsequent row (i.e., $2 in the second row, $3 in the third, and so on, up to $12 in the twelfth row).

    Example:


    You can try out the example task at the bottom of this page.
  4. Payment for Part 3: At the end of the survey, one of your choices (one row) will be randomly selected to calculate your Part 3 bonus.
    1. If you chose to hire the worker (option 1) in that selected row, you earn $1 × the worker’s actual score.

      Example:
      Suppose this worker’s actual score was 5. Because you chose “Hire,” you earn $5.
    2. If you chose the fixed amount (option 2) in that selected row, you get that amount, no matter what the worker’s score is.

      Example:
      Since you chose $7 in the selected row, you earn $7, regardless of the worker’s score.
  5. How to make your choices: Option 1 (on the left) is pre-selected by default. You only need to indicate where you want to “cross” from option 1 to option 2. Below your crossover point, the rows will autofill to option 2. There is no right answer, but make sure your choices reflect your true preferences.

    Example:

    You should only have one “crossover” row. Think about why you’d only cross once.

  6. Note:
    • Your choices do not affect the earnings of the workers.
    • Your choices do affect your bonus for Part 3.
