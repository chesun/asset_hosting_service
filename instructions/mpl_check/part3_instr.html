Instructions

Overview: In this part, you’ll consider hiring decisions for a set of workers. After reading these instructions, you’ll answer a short comprehension quiz (2 attempts per question). Then, you’ll make your hiring choices that can affect your bonus.

  1. Random Worker Profiles: You will see 8 workers, each randomly chosen from our group (each worker has an equal chance of being selected).

    Each worker’s profile includes:
    • A nickname
    • A profile avatar
    • A short description
    • Some profiles include the worker's score range, other do not

    Example Profile:

  2. Your Task: For each worker, you’ll make 12 choices shown in the example below. Each row is a choice between 2 options:
    • Hire the worker (option 1, on the left), and receive $1 for every point the worker scored (out of 12).
    • Don't hire the worker (option 2, on the right), and receive a fixed amount of money shown for each row.

    Example:


    You can try out the example task at the bottom of this page.
  3. Payment for Part 3: At the end of the survey, one of your choices (one row) will be randomly selected to calculate your Part 3 bonus.
    1. If you chose to hire the worker (option 1) in that selected row, you earn $1 × the worker’s actual score.

      Example:
      Suppose this worker’s actual score was 5. Because you chose “Hire,” you earn $5.
    2. If you chose the fixed amount (option 2) in that selected row, you get that amount, no matter what the worker’s score is.

      Example:
      Since you chose $7 in the selected row, you earn $7, regardless of the worker’s score.
  4. How to make your choices: Option 1 (on the left) is pre-selected by default. You only need to indicate where you want to “cross” from option 1 to option 2. Below your crossover point, the rows will autofill to option 2. There is no right answer, but make sure your choices reflect your true preferences.

    Example:

    You should only have one “crossover” row. Think about why you’d only cross once.

  5. Note:
    • Your choices do not affect the earnings of the workers.
    • Your choices do affect your bonus for Part 3.
