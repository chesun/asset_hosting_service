Part 3 Instructions

Part 3 Overview: In this final part, you’ll consider hiring decisions for a set of workers. After reading these instructions, you’ll answer a short comprehension quiz (2 attempts per question). Then, you’ll make your hiring choices that can affect your bonus.

  1. Random Worker Profiles: You will see ${e://Field/n_profiles} workers, each randomly chosen from our group (each worker has an equal chance of being selected).

    Each worker’s profile includes:
    • A nickname
    • A profile avatar
    • A short description

    Example Profile:

  2. Your Task: For each worker, you’ll make a series of choices between:
    • Hiring the worker (left option), which pays you $1 for every point the worker scored (out of 12).
    • Receiving a fixed amount of money (right option), shown for each row.

    Example:

  3. Payment for Part 3: At the end of the survey, one of your choices (one row) will be randomly selected to calculate your Part 3 bonus.
    1. If you chose to hire the worker (the left option) in that selected row, you earn $1 × the worker’s actual score.

      Example:
      Suppose this worker’s actual score was 2. Because you chose “Hire,” you earn $2.
    2. If you chose the fixed amount (right option) in that selected row, you get that amount, no matter what the worker’s score is.

      Example:
      If you chose $7 in the selected row, you earn $7, regardless of the worker’s score.
  4. How to make your choices: The left option is pre-selected by default. You only need to indicate where you want to “switch” from the left option to the right option. Below your switch point, the rows will autofill to the right option.

    Example:

    Once you switch, do not switch back. Think about why you’d only switch once.

  5. Note:
    • Your choices do not affect the earnings of the workers.
    • Your choices do affect your bonus for Part 3.
