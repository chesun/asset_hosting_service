Part 3 Instructions

 
  1. Please pay attention to the following instructions. As a reminder, we will ask you some comprehension check questions about the Part 3 instructions.
    • If you answer these questions correctly on the first try you will receive a 1 EC bonus.
    • You have 2 attempts for each question.
  2. In Part 3, you will play the role of an employer who is hiring workers.
  3. You have 12 EC in your "account" to use in this part.
  4. You will be presented with 4 workers who are randomly drawn from our group of workers. You will see a nickname, profile avatar, and a short profile for each worker.
    Example Profile:
     
  5. For each worker, your task is to make a wage offer (between 0 and 12 EC). The wage offer is accepted or rejected by the following algorithm:
    • A random "asking salary" between 0 and 12 is randomly generated by the computer.
    • If the asking salary is less than or equal to your wage offer, the offer is accepted. You pay the asking salary to hire the worker, and receive 1 EC for every question the worker answered correctly. i.e. your profit = worker's score - asking salary.
    • If the asking salary is greater than your wage offer, the offer is rejected. You receive no profit.
    • Your total profit = profit from wage offer + money left in your account
  6. The following are a few example scenarios of how the algorithm works:
    • Example 1:
      • Your wage offer is 6 and the worker's score is 9.
      • The asking salary is 2.
      • Since asking salary < wage offer, your offer is accepted. 
      • You earn a profit of worker score - asking salary = 9 - 2 = 7
      • Your total profit is (12 - 2) + 7 =17 EC
    • Example 2:
      • Your wage offer is $9 and the worker's score is 10.
      • The asking salary is 11.
      • Since asking salary > wage offer, your offer is rejected.
      • Your total profit is 0.
    • Example 3:
      • Your wage offer is $8 and the worker's score is 4.
      • The asking salary is 6.
      • Since asking salary < wage offer, your offer is accepted.
      • You earn a profit of worker score - asking salary = 4 - 6 = -2 EC.
      • Your total profit is (12 - 6) + -2 = 4 EC
  7. The wage offers you make do not affect the earnings of the workers. 
  8. At the end of the survey, 20% of participants will be randomly selected to be paid a bonus. If you are selected to be paid a bonus, one of the questions will be randomly selected and your answer is used to determine your bonus.
    • If the selected question is a wage offer in Part 3, then your bonus will be your total profit in that question
    • Your bonus is converted to USD (1 EC = 0.5 USD) at the end of the experiment and paid to you within one week of completion
