  1. In Part 2, you will answer the same 2 questions as in Part 1. However, this time you will receive some information about the workers to help you answer the questions. This is in the form of a graph, which shows you how many workers scored in a given interval.

  2. The information you learn in Part 2 will be helpful for completing your tasks better in Part 3.
  3. At the end of the experiment, we will randomly select 20% of participants to be paid a bonus based on their choices.
  4. If you are selected to be paid a bonus, we will randomly select one question from the survey and use your answer to determine your bouns. If you are selected to be paid a bonus, and the selected question is in Part 2:
    1. If the question is to guess the number of workers with each possible score:
      One of your answers in this question is randomly selected, and you are paid a 8 EC bonus if your answer is within ±2 of the correct answer. Otherwise you are paid 0.
    2. If the question is to guess the average score/age:
      You are paid a 8 EC bonus if your answer is within ±2 of the correct answer. Otherwise you are paid 0.
